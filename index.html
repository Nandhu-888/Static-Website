Charminer

The Charminar, a prominent landmark in Hyderabad, India, is said to have been built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1591. While various beliefs and theories surround its construction, the most popular story suggests it was built to celebrate the end of a plague that had devastated the city.
